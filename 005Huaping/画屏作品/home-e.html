Literati painting 

Literati painting, also known as "the scholar-official painting", "soffor painting", the content of ancient art education. Refers to the paintings of Chinese and scholar-officials in Chinese feudal society. Unlike the paintings of folk and court paintings. Beginning in the Tang Dynasty Wang Wei. The author generally avoids social reality, and draws more from landscapes and flowers and trees to express his own "sexual spirit", and also contains resentment against national oppression or decadent politics. It is advertised as "morality" and "yipin", which emphasizes the taste of pen and ink, deviates from the shape, emphasizes the charm, and attaches importance to the cultivation of calligraphy and literature and the expression of artistic conception in painting. It has an impact on the development of traditional aesthetic education and the development of techniques such as ink painting and freehand brushwork. In modern times, Chen Hengyi believed that "literature painting has four elements: character, learning, talent and thought. With these four, it can be perfected."

Flower and bird painting

Flower and bird paintings are paintings of Chinese paintings, which are painted with flowers, birds, and insects. In Chinese painting, paintings depicting flowers, flowers, birds, and fish worms are called flower and bird paintings. There are three kinds of paintings in the flower and bird paintings: "gongbi", "freehand", and "working with writing". The meticulous flower and bird paintings use thick and light ink to outline the objects, and then shade the layers in depth; the freehand flower and bird paintings use simple and general methods to draw the objects; between the meticulous and freehand, it is called the work and writing, and the form is realistic. Chinese flower and bird paintings are one of the three major painting disciplines in China. The objects depicted by flowers and birds are actually not just flowers and birds, but all kinds of animals and plants, including flowers, fruits and vegetables, manes, grass worms, animals and so on.

a kind of painting. The general name of painting with the character image as the main body. Chinese figure painting, referred to as "character", is a large painting in Chinese painting. It appears earlier than landscape painting, flower and bird painting; it is roughly divided into Taoist paintings, ladies paintings, portraits, genre paintings, historical story paintings, etc. The figure painting strives to make the characters' personalities vivid and vivid, with vivid charm and both form and spirit. Its expressive method often expresses the character's character in the environment, atmosphere, body and dynamic rendering.
